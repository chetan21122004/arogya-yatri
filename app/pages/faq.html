General Questions

Arogya Yatri is a comprehensive medical tourism facilitation service that connects international patients with world-class hospitals and specialist doctors in India. We provide end-to-end support for your medical journey, from initial consultation to post-treatment follow-up care, while also helping you explore India's rich culture and wellness traditions.
Arogya Yatri is a service by Ewan Business Solutions, a company specializing in multilingual support and global healthcare coordination. We combine medical expertise with cultural sensitivity to ensure seamless healthcare experiences for international patients.
We serve patients from all countries worldwide. Our multilingual support covers 120+ languages, and we have extensive experience working with patients from the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, Middle East, Europe, Africa, and Southeast Asian countries.
We work with a network of NABH and JCI-accredited hospitals across India. We are an independent medical tourism facilitator, which means we provide unbiased recommendations based on your specific medical needs and can connect you with the best-suited hospitals and specialists.

Cost & Pricing

You can save up to 80% compared to treatment costs in the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, and many European countries. For example, a cardiac bypass surgery that costs $100,000+ in the US may cost around $7,000-$10,000 in India, including hospital stay and basic accommodation.
No. We believe in complete transparency. Our cost estimates include treatment fees, hospital charges, doctor fees, diagnostic tests, and medications during your hospital stay. We'll clearly outline any additional costs such as travel, accommodation, and post-treatment medications upfront.
Our cost estimate typically includes: pre-treatment consultations and diagnostic tests, the medical procedure itself, surgeon and anesthetist fees, hospital room charges, nursing care, medications during hospital stay, follow-up consultations during your India stay, and basic medical supplies. Accommodation and travel costs are quoted separately.
We accept international wire transfers, credit/debit cards, and online payment platforms. Payment is made directly to the hospital for medical services. Our facilitation fees and other service charges can be paid through multiple convenient methods. Detailed payment instructions will be provided during your booking process.
Payment terms vary by hospital and treatment type. Many hospitals accept payment in phases (advance deposit, pre-surgery payment, and final settlement). For specific financing options, please discuss with our care coordinators during your consultation, and we'll help explore available options.

Medical Visa & Travel

An e-Medical visa is a special visa category introduced by the Indian government under the "Heal in India" program for international patients seeking medical treatment. We provide complete assistance with the application process, helping you prepare necessary documents including your medical reports, hospital invitation letter, and passport copies.
E-Medical visas are typically processed within 3-5 business days. However, we recommend applying at least 2-3 weeks before your planned travel to account for any potential delays. Our team will guide you through the entire process and track your application status.
Yes! We encourage patients to travel with a companion. Under the e-Medical visa program, up to two companions can accompany you on e-Medical Attendant visas. We provide free travel arrangement assistance for one companion, including visa support and accommodation coordination.
Our representative will be waiting for you at the airport arrival area with a name placard. We provide fast-track airport assistance, help with immigration if needed, and escort you to your pre-arranged transportation. If you require wheelchair assistance or have special needs, we arrange that in advance as well.

Post-Treatment Care

Our care doesn't end when you leave India. We maintain continuous follow-up through remote consultations, monitor your recovery progress, coordinate with your local doctors if needed, arrange medicine delivery, and remain available for any questions or concerns during your recovery period.
